Saving the ocean starts on your own. Make these changes to your lifestyle today!

Why 3R? It’s easy: although we cannot completely erase all the plastics at this moment due to our current technology, we can limit the amount of plastic we manufacture and use. It’s like having a diet: if you wanna lose some weight, eat less! If you wanna save our oceans, use less plastics, so they are less likely to end up in our oceans. Why reuse? Same thing-- if we already practice limiting the amount of plastic products manufactured, why don’t we reuse them? As always, recycle them so they don’t end up killing wildlife.

Why manufacture plastics or other non-recyclable products when we can use sustainable products? Here’s a simple way to ask fast: when you go to your local Chinese fast food restaurants for some chow meins, don’t take their bamboo chopsticks or plastic forks. Instead, you can grab some metal chopsticks and forks. Similarly, why spend a dollar on a water bottle when you can purchase a reusable bottle? Not only are you drinking water from your own house, you can also wash them, dry them, and be a good vsco person.

It’s obvious: you are breaking the law and ruining the scenery. Be a law abiding citizen smh.

Not only you can help your community clean up local water, it is also an amazing opportunity to take a selfie next to the sunsetting beach and use the hashtag #savetheturtles. It is a typical win-win situation. You are helping the ocean, and you are also gaining followers on Instagram from your amazing photography skills. Modern problems require modern solutions; be a smart kid and clean your local beach.

Mar Alliance

As an organization, MarAlliance improves the understanding and conservation of threatened marine species and their habitats, notably sharks and rays on the Mesoamerican reef. To do this, they create new knowledge, train local fishermen with this knowledge, and share this knowledge in many different formats to many different audiences, from the youngest audiences in pre-schools all the way to politicians and other decision-makers. Through this, They hope to inspire a sense of wonder about the ocean, to promote sustainable tourism and to foster the effectiveness of marine protected areas.

4Ocean

4Ocean is a business that is here to clean the ocean and coastlines while working to stop the inflow of plastic by changing consumption habits. They were born on a plastic-covered beach in Bali, Indonesia. Upom visiting Bali for the surf trip of their life time, the founders of 4Ocean found a beach that was completely covered in plastic, with trash-filled waves delivering more garbage with each break. They were surprised at what they saw, yet were told that the beaches had been cleaned just hours earlier. The trash they were wading through had only just washed ashore. Their eyes were immediately opened to the magnitude of the ocean plastic crisis, and they vowed on the spot to try to do something about it.

Project Aware

Project AWARE takes action to create both local and global change for the ocean and the communities who depend on it, they connect the passion for ocean adventure with the purpose of marine conservation. Their vision is to return to a clean, healthy ocean. There are two critical focus areas for Project AWARE: Community and Policy. When both are working together, they are driving towards our mission and vision. As for current focus areas, Project AWARE develops actions under their Clean Ocean and Healthy Ocean programs each year. In 2019, they supported our community and advancing policies on Plastic Debris and Sharks & Rays. In terms of impact, together with our partners and global community, Project AWARE responded to the threats our ocean faces and they’ve had some incredible successes for over 25 years.
